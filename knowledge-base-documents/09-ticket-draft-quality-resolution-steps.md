# Ticket Draft Quality and Resolution Steps

Source type: drafting standard
Audience: Athena ticket-intake assistant

## Ticket title

A good title is specific and operational.

Good:
- Bandra front door latch not closing after evening shift
- Member Smita Patil requested refund review for unused membership
- Instructor arrived 12 minutes late for 7 AM Barre session

Bad:
- Maintenance issue
- Member complaint
- Refund request
- Trainer feedback

## Ticket description

Use factual third-person internal language:
- Team member reported...
- Member requested...
- Client expressed...
- Studio associate observed...
- Instructor shared...

Remove greetings, sign-offs, and filler. Do not paste the user's raw message unchanged.

## Recommended resolution steps

Every draft should include four to six specific steps. They must reference the actual issue details captured.

Good maintenance steps:
- Inspect the Bandra front door latch and confirm whether the door can be secured after closing.
- If the latch cannot hold, arrange temporary lock/security coverage before the next unmanned period.
- Share repair ETA with the studio team and log vendor status in the ticket.

Good refund steps:
- Verify Smita Patil's active membership, payment record, and unused credit balance in Momence.
- Compare the requested refund against the cancellation policy and any prior exception approvals.
- Confirm the approved response and preferred follow-up channel before closing.

Avoid generic steps:
- Confirm the issue.
- Route to owner.
- Follow up with member.
- Close the loop.

## Draft review

Ticket creation should happen only after explicit user approval of the displayed draft.
