# Routing and SLA Rules

Source type: routing reference
Audience: Athena ticket-intake assistant

## Priority and SLA

Critical:
- SLA target: 2 hours
- Use for safety, access risk, harassment, theft, injury, medical issue, live operational shutdown, or high-risk escalation.

High:
- SLA target: 8 hours
- Use for service failures, member retention risk, urgent refunds, live class impact, unresolved complaints, trainer no-show, or visible business disruption.

Medium:
- SLA target: 24 hours
- Use for normal operational issues, requests, follow-ups, and non-critical member experience issues.

Low:
- SLA target: 72 hours
- Use for cosmetic issues, informational notes, record-only documentation, compliments, and low-risk improvements.

## Department routing principles

Pricing and Memberships, Billing and Membership, Scheduling, Booking and Schedule, Sales and Consultation:
- Route to Sales and Client Servicing unless local studio ownership clearly applies.

Repair and Maintenance, Studio Amenities and Facilities, Facility and Equipment, Safety and Security, App and Digital, Operating Systems:
- Route to Operations, Maintenance, or Technical Support based on issue type.

Trainer Feedback, Instructor and Class Quality, Class Experience, Member Progress and Transformation:
- Route to Training and Client Experience.

Customer Service and Communication, Front Desk and Service:
- Route to Client Success / Front Desk ownership.

Hosted Class and Partnerships:
- Route to partnerships, brand, sales, or client success depending on whether the issue is partner quality, lead conversion, guest feedback, or event execution.

## Routing questions

Ask for studio only when it affects ownership, trend reporting, physical location, or local follow-up.

Ask for member only when a specific person must be contacted, compensated, verified, or linked to Momence.

Ask for session only when a specific class, booking, trainer, attendance record, or class impact must be verified.
