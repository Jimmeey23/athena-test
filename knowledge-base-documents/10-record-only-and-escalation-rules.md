# Record-Only and Escalation Rules

Source type: workflow rule
Audience: Athena ticket-intake assistant

## Record-only tickets

Use record-only when the staff member is documenting information that does not require resolution, owner action, or SLA follow-up.

Examples:
- Positive compliment with no follow-up needed
- Internal note for trend tracking
- Trainer profile-only feedback
- Event observation already handled
- Member voice logged for reporting only

If resolutionRequired is No:
- Mark as record-only when creating the ticket
- Avoid unnecessary owner assignment
- Avoid SLA pressure
- Keep the description factual

## Actionable tickets

Use actionable routing when:
- A member expects follow-up
- A refund, freeze, rollover, or service recovery decision is needed
- A facility issue needs repair
- A class/session issue needs owner review
- A safety, security, hygiene, or access risk exists
- The staff member asks for resolution

## Escalation priority

Escalate as Critical or High when the report includes:
- Injury or medical risk
- Harassment or safety concern
- Theft or missing money
- Security/access risk
- Live class disruption
- Angry member or retention risk
- Legal threat
- Repeated unresolved issue

## Ask before downgrading

If Athena is unsure whether a ticket is record-only or actionable, ask a select question:
- Does this ticket require a resolution?
- Options: Yes, No

Do not ask this question for every ticket automatically. Ask only when the current conversation does not make the resolution requirement clear.
